Chapter 3 Data Management

By far the primary method of managing data involves the use of Emory Box although no one interviwed believes this to be ideal ordesirable outside of its approved use for hosting health data. The data will typically be distributed out to analysts and collaborators though intermediate and final results are rarely stored alongside the original data or in a manner that facilitates reproducibility. Some faculty do use R Notebooks but point to the problem of keeping track of results generated by other who might touch the project and preserve their own scripts and transformed data on their laptop or shared drive. also work on the project. Given that shuffling data around is a manual process it is done with no specific frequency or in accordance with any standard thus “working back” from results to the original data is challenging. Some data resides in Redcap though the concerns about reproducibility remain given that results aren’t generally hosted alongside the raw data used to initiate the study.
